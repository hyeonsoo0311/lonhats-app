# Product Direction

## One Sentence

Lonhats is a record-based community app for people who quietly try to become better than yesterday.

## What Lonhats Is

Lonhats helps users inspect and cultivate their lives through simple records. The product works like a personal thermometer and hygrometer: it shows the user's current life temperature and humidity based on the things they actually did and felt.

The app should make small effort visible. A walk after work, a meal without late-night snacking, a full night's sleep, one page of reading, or an honest reflection can all count as `Today's Better`.

## What Lonhats Is Not

Lonhats is not:

- A precision workout tracker.
- A bodybuilding log optimized around sets, reps, and progressive overload.
- A strict calorie-control app.
- A transformation ranking community.
- A public leaderboard.
- A shame-based habit app.

Workout, food, calorie, and exercise data can exist, but they are supporting signals. They should not take over the product's emotional center.

## Core Loop

The core loop is `Today's Better`.

1. The user records a small proof of effort in one of four stacks.
2. The user adds a short meaning or reflection.
3. Lonhats turns the records into a simple view of life direction.
4. The user may share a small proof with the community.
5. The community responds with belonging, not comparison.

## Four Stacks

### Move Stack

Movement and body activation. Examples: walking, running, health training, yoga, pilates, home training, stretching, other movement.

### Meal Stack

Food and meal rhythm. The focus is not perfect dieting. The focus is whether the user fed themselves in a way that supported the day.

### Recovery Stack

Sleep, rest, fatigue, physical care, and recovery. This stack protects the product from becoming output-only.

### Mind Stack

Reading, studying, writing, reflection, projects, and quiet self-development.

## Community Direction

The community is for `small proof`, not performance comparison.

Users should be encouraged to share records like:

- "I almost lay down after work, but I walked for 20 minutes."
- "It was not perfect, but I skipped late-night snacks today."
- "I read only three pages, but I kept the promise."

The community should make users feel:

- "Other people are also trying quietly."
- "My small effort counts."
- "I can continue without pretending to be perfect."

## Product Tone

- Quiet.
- Warm.
- Direct.
- Not childish.
- Not overly motivational.
- Never shaming.

Lonhats should speak like a calm witness to the user's effort.

## Strategic Priority

Prioritize the recording and sharing loop before advanced analytics.

If a feature makes recording slower, more judgmental, or more comparison-driven, it should be simplified or rejected.
